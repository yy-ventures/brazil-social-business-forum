"All humans are born entrepreneurs"

Professor Muhammad Yunus

2006 Nobel Peace Laureate

Brazil Social Business Forum on Youth Entrepreneurship 2024 will be a transformative event that aims to address two pressing challenges Brazil is facing today: youth unemployment and climate protection. As a country with great potential but facing social and environmental issues, social businesses can offer a proven way to tackle these challenges in Brazil. The forum will serve as a platform to explore how the principles of social business can not only provide avenues for tackling youth unemployment, but also empower young entrepreneurs to lead the charge in safeguarding our planet's delicate climate balance.




Brazil, the world's fifth-largest country in terms of area with a population of over 215 million, faces the challenge of creating jobs for its growing number of young adults, with a median age of 32.4 years as of 2020.

The conventional approach to job creation often needs to address the multifaceted dimensions of this challenge. Social Business, which Nobel Peace Laureate Professor Muhammad Yunus coined, offers a unique approach to addressing social and environmental problems that differs from the usual way of doing business. It involves not only providing financial support, but also building a network of like-minded individuals who share a vision for a better future.

To provide an inclusive space for entrepreneurs, young people, ecosystem builders, policymakers and investors to collaborate and identify key challenges and opportunities in the social business sector, the Brazil Social Business Forum on Youth Entrepreneurship 2024 will be a one or two-day-long event that will bring together a flourishing community of like-minded individuals who share a common goal of developing sustainable social business solutions to tackle Brazil's urgent social and environmental challenges.
